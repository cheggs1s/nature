For a brighter future

Introduction

Our mission is to educate people about the importance of protecting our planet and its ecosystems. We are committed to raising awareness about the environmental challenges occuring everyday, which will hopefully insprite sustainable actions and foster a deep respect for nature among our community.



What's happening to our Earth?

  1. 2024 marked the hottest year on record, continuing a trend where the five warmest years have all occurred since 2015.
  2. Satellite data reveals that glaciers worldwide are retreating at an accelerated pace, contributing to rising sea levels.
  3. Deforestation continues at an alarming rate, particularly in tropical regions.


History of our Earth

Year 1750: Pre-Industrial nature thrived.

1850: Industrial Revolution begins with heavy emissions.

1900: Urbanization accelerates.

1950: Post-war industrial boom increases energy consumption dramatically.

1980: Environmental movements gain momentum worldwide.

2000: Renewable energy technologies begin wider adoption.

Importance of biodiversity

Biodiversity is the heartbeat of our planet, encompassing the intricate web of life from the tiniest microbes to towering trees. Its preservation is paramount, as biodiversity sustains ecosystems, provides essential resources, and enriches human lives culturally and economically.



Know your endangered friends

Endangered Animals

Giant Panda

Status: Vulnerable
Habitat: Mountainous regions of central China
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and fragmentation
Conservation Efforts: Breeding programs and habitat protection


Amur Leopard

Status: Critically Endangered
Habitat: Russian Far East and Northeast China
Threats: Poaching and habitat destruction
Population: ~100 left in the wild


Dama Gazelle

Status: Critically Endangered
Habitat: Sahara and Sahel (Africa)
Threats: Overhunting, habitat loss
Range: Chad, Mali, Niger


Galápagos Penguin

Status: Endangered
Habitat: Galápagos Islands
Threats: Climate change, human disturbance
Note: Only penguin species north of the equator


Vaquita

Status: Critically Endangered
Habitat: Northern Gulf of California, Mexico
Threats: Fishing bycatch
Population: Fewer than 10 remaining


Javan Rhinoceros

Status: Critically Endangered
Habitat: Java, Indonesia
Threats: Habitat loss, poaching
Population: Around 60 individuals


California Condor

Status: Critically Endangered
Habitat: Southwestern U.S.
Threats: Lead poisoning, habitat destruction
Conservation: Captive breeding and reintroduction


Chinese Alligator

Status: Critically Endangered
Habitat: Eastern China
Threats: Habitat loss, pollution
Population: ~10,000 (mostly in captivity)


Red Panda

Status: Endangered
Habitat: Eastern Himalayas and southwestern China
Threats: Deforestation, poaching, habitat fragmentation
